World Health Organization

Model United Nations Conference

1. Introduction

The World Health Organization (WHO) committee in a Model UN simulates the World Health Assembly, focusing on global public health governance, disease prevention, and health systems strengthening.

2. Mandate & Objectives

  • Global Health Policy: Develop international health regulations and guidelines.
  • Disease Prevention & Control: Address communicable and non-communicable diseases.
  • Health Emergencies: Coordinate responses to pandemics and health crises.
  • Technical Cooperation: Provide capacity-building to Member States and health agencies.

3. Committee Structure

  • Membership: All WHO Member States.
  • Dais: Chair, Vice-Chairs.

4. Typical Session Flow

  1. Roll Call & Agenda Adoption
  2. General Debate
  3. Interactive Hearings: With experts and UN health bodies.
  4. Caucusing: Moderated & Unmoderated discussions.
  5. Drafting Resolutions

5. Draft Resolution Components

  • Preambulatory Clauses: Cite the WHO Constitution, International Health Regulations, and past Assembly decisions.
  • Operative Clauses: Specific health interventions, timelines, funding sources, and monitoring mechanisms.

7. Delegate Preparation & Strategy

  • Research your country's public health priorities and WHO policies.
  • Review WHO reports, health statistics, and expert recommendations.
  • Form alliances with countries sharing health challenges.
  • Consult NGOs and health institutions for evidence-based solutions.

8. Expected Outcomes

  • Resolutions promoting effective health interventions and policies.
  • Improved understanding of global health governance.
  • Strengthened diplomatic collaboration on health issues.

9. Conclusion

Participation in the WHO committee allows delegates to engage in critical health policymaking, enhancing their skills in negotiation and understanding of global health challenges.
